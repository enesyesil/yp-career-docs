# Career Academy Program

The Career Academy is our long-term main support program designed to help students turn confusion into clarity, and effort into structured progress. 


It combines mentoring, planning, and accountability through a guided process that adapts to each student’s goals and circumstances.

---

## 1. Purpose

Career Academy exists to:

- Give students **direction** when they feel lost or overwhelmed.
- Build **systems and habits** that create long-term stability.
- Translate vague goals (“I want a better career”) into **clear weekly actions**.
- Provide a supportive environment where progress is tracked, celebrated, and adjusted.

Many students don’t fail due to lack of motivation — they fail due to lack of clarity, consistency, or structure.  
Career Academy solves exactly that.

---

## 2. Who It Is For

Career Academy is designed for students who:

- Want structured support.
- Need help balancing school, work, and life stress etc.
- Are preparing for future careers and want guidance on skill-building and planning.
- Are committed to showing up, being honest, and taking small consistent steps.

**Not for:**  
Mental health crises, legal issues, or specialized professional help — we refer out to our other departments under Young professionals Canada when necessary.

---

## 3. Program Flow

Career Academy follows a predictable structure so students always know where they are in their journey.

### **Step 1 — Scouting**
Initial conversation.  
Purpose: understand background, goals, and immediate struggles.

### **Step 2 — Diagnosis**
A caseworker uses our structured questions, forms and checklists to identify:
- Primary blockers  
- Support needs  
- Suitable pathways  

Diagnosis determines where a student should focus first.

### **Step 3 — Pathways**
Every student receives a tailored combination of the following major pathways:

1. **Foundation Pathway**  
   - Build daily routines  
   - Improve discipline  
   - Fix scheduling, study habits, and consistency  

2. **Strategy Pathway**  
   - Career direction  
   - Decision-making  
   - Long-term goal mapping  

3. **Candidacy Pathway**  
   - Resume and LinkedIn  
   - Projects and experience  
   - Job applications, preparation, mock interviews  

4. **Experience Pathway**  
   - Volunteering and internships  
   - Small projects
   - Networking  
   - Exposure to real work environments  

### **Step 4 — Weekly/Biweekly Rhythm**
Each mentee has regular meetings using our standard **meeting template**, including:
- Review of last week  
- Discussion of challenges  
- Setting 1–3 realistic tasks  
- Logging the meeting in the system  

### **Step 5 — Review Cycle**
Every 8–12 weeks:
- Re-evaluate progress  
- Update pathways  
- Adjust meeting frequency  
- Decide if the student should enter:
  - A new pathway,
  - Or graduation from the program

---

## 4. Roles

### **Mentee**
- Shows up consistently  
- Communicates openly
- Completes tasks (or updates caseworker)  
- Tracks commitments and deadlines 
- Understands that meaningful growth takes time and agrees to a **long-term commitment (ideally 1–2 years or more)**, depending on goals and circumstances 

### **Caseworker**
- Provides structure, planning, and accountability  
- Uses logs, checklists, and templates  
- Helps mentee focus on one main direction at a time  
- Coordinates with professionals when needed  

### **Professionals**
- Provide targeted guidance (industry, skill, feedback)  
- Support caseworkers during advanced or specialized sessions  

---

## 5. Principles of Career Academy

1. **Small Steps Beat Big Plans**  
   We prioritize realistic weekly tasks over overwhelming long-term lists.

2. **Structure Creates Stability**  
   Meetings, logs, and pathways reduce confusion and help mentees stay grounded.

3. **Flexibility Without Drift**  
   The program adapts to life changes while keeping a clear direction.

4. **Documentation Matters**  
   Logs, templates, and notes ensure continuity and transparency.

5. **Support, Not Pressure**  
   The environment is human, respectful, and avoids judgment.

---

## 6. Typical Student Journey

A simplified example of how a student progresses:

**Months 1–2:**  
Foundation + Strategy  
- Fix routine, clarify goals, set direction  

**Months 3–4:**  
Candidacy  
- Resume, LinkedIn, projects, applications  

**Months 5+**  
Experience  
- Internships, volunteering, practical exposure  

Not all journeys look identical, but this pattern helps set expectations.

---

## 7. Enrolment Steps

To bring a student into Career Academy:

1. Conduct initial intake conversation  
2. Add student to the system  
3. Schedule a full Diagnosis session  
4. Select their primary pathway(s)  
5. Set meeting frequency  
6. Assign first 2–3 tasks  
7. Start logs and regular follow-ups

Career Academy is the backbone of our support model.  
It is structured enough to guide students — and flexible enough to serve real life.
