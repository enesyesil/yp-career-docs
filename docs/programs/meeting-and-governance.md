# Meeting & Governance Structure

This section defines how Career Academy coordinates work, makes decisions,
and maintains alignment across the system.

These meetings are operational.
They exist to ensure continuity, accountability, and coordination.

---

## Purpose

The Meeting & Governance Structure exists to:

- review system activity,
- make matching and assignment decisions,
- coordinate events and resources,
- ensure alignment across roles,
- maintain institutional continuity.

Meetings are not optional check-ins.
They are part of how the system functions.

---

## 1. Weekly Admin / Board Meeting

**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Participants:** Director, General Secretary, relevant leads

### Focus Areas
- New mentees
- New Caseworkers
- Matching decisions
- Professional network growth
- Events planning
- Resource production

### Guidelines
- Come prepared with updates
- Focus on decisions and blockers
- Avoid operational details that belong in other meetings
- Log decisions and action items

---

## 2. Monthly Caseworker Meeting

**Frequency:** Monthly  
**Participants:** Caseworkers, Caseworker Director

### Purpose
- Development and learning
- Experience sharing
- Alignment on expectations and goals

### Guidelines
- Share challenges and patterns
- Discuss what is working and what is not
- Align on checklist usage and follow-up quality
- Keep the space practical and respectful

This meeting is for improvement, not evaluation.

---

## 3. Annual Community Gathering

**Frequency:** Once per year  
**Participants:** Mentees, Caseworkers, Professionals

### Purpose
- Community connection
- Relationship strengthening
- Visibility of the system as a whole

### Guidelines
- Keep the tone inclusive and accessible
- Focus on shared experience, not hierarchy
- Use the gathering to reinforce long-term engagement
