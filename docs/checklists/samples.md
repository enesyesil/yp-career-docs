# Checklist Samples

This page provides sample checklists currently used in Career Academy.

These checklists are practical working tools.
They may be adapted based on context, stage, and mentee needs,
but their structure and intent remain consistent.

---

## Sample: Experience Checklist

This checklist is used to support mentees in building real-world exposure,
academic stability, and professional presence.

### Leadership & Engagement
- Hold an executive or leadership role in a student club or initiative
- Identify at least 5 active clubs through the university
- Attend club events and speak with at least 2 executives
- Learn club hiring timelines

### Academic Planning
- Maintain good academic standing
- Draft a semester plan
- Draft a graduation plan (prioritizing academic responsibilities)
- Submit both plans for review

### Internships & Exposure
- Research internship opportunities in the relevant field
- Prepare a list of companies hiring
- Apply to internships
- Complete at least one internship when possible

### Events & Workshops
- Attend workshops and events
- Track relevant workshops via the university website

### Faculty Relationships
- Attend office hours
- Participate during lectures (at least one question or comment)
- Send a thank-you email after completing a course

### Career Mapping
- Write down possible career paths in the field
- Prioritize options
- Meet with at least one professional in each field to discuss requirements and work environment

### Campus Resources
- Identify school resources (writing centre, advisors, registrar, etc.)
- Apply for scholarships and awards
- Submit a list of useful resources

### Professional Presence
- Create or update LinkedIn profile
- Obtain a professional headshot
- Attend LinkedIn workshops
- Reorganize LinkedIn profile

### Networking
- Reach out to at least 3 professionals on LinkedIn (prioritizing alumni)
- Schedule coffee chats
- Build peer connections with classmates or club members

### Teaching / Research Roles (If Applicable)
- Explore TA or RA opportunities
- Identify relevant professors
- Attend office hours and network
- Reach out by email regarding opportunities


## Sample: Computer Science – Academic & Career Growth Checklist

This checklist is used when a mentee has a **clear Computer Science direction**
and requests structured, field-specific preparation.

It is applied **in addition to** the mentee’s assigned stage checklist.

---

### Stage 1 — Foundations

- Create a LinkedIn profile
- Create a professional resume  
  - Have it reviewed by at least 3 credible engineers
- Build a basic portfolio website
- Identify the university job or co-op portal
- Apply to internship postings
- Maintain a GPA of 3.2 or higher
- Apply to at least one TA position

---

### Stage 2 — Skill Building

- Complete a Data Structures & Algorithms course with strong performance
- Solve at least 40 LeetCode questions
- Create and maintain at least one personal project on GitHub
- Attend at least 3 hackathons and submit a project
- Apply to additional internships
- Follow relevant hiring or job-posting accounts on LinkedIn

---

### Stage 3 — Experience & Positioning

- Complete at least one four-month internship
- Increase total solved LeetCode questions to approximately 120
- Complete the LeetCode Blind 75 list
- Apply to additional internships
- Maintain at least three side projects or one large project on GitHub
- Deploy at least one web project  
  (e.g. GitHub Pages, Netlify, Vercel)
- Connect with at least 10 recruiters on LinkedIn
- Research and document hiring strategies of multiple companies

---

### Stage 4 — Advanced Readiness

- Complete at least two internships in total
- Increase total solved LeetCode questions to approximately 200
- Apply to new graduate roles
- Contribute to a mid-to-large open-source project  
  (multiple merged pull requests)
- Obtain referrals from industry professionals

---

### Stage 5 — Post-Graduation (Ongoing)

- Apply to positions weekly
- Contribute regularly to open-source projects
- Practice technical problems weekly
- Push commits to personal projects consistently
- Hold regular coffee chats or reviews with professionals
- Attend recruitment or career information sessions monthly


## Summary

The sample checklists in this section illustrate how Career Academy uses
checklists to support structured progress.

They are meant to:
- clarify expectations,
- break goals into actionable steps,
- support consistent follow-up by Caseworkers,
- and help mentees move forward at a realistic pace.
